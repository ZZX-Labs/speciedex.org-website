Decentralized Biodiversity Data Protocol

Speciedex

Speciedex is an open biodiversity protocol, dataset, and distributed public infrastructure for collecting, validating, preserving, studying, and serving the documented record of non-human life.

Every permitted species is assigned a singular blockchain. That chain stores the ordered history of data submissions, evidence, revisions, disputes, validation outcomes, and accepted scientific assertions for that species.

Bitcoin is used for all monetary compensation, including validated contributions and data-bounty work. Speciedex has no native speculative token.

Homo sapiens is permanently forbidden from entry into the Speciedex system by protocol design.

Protocol Model

A Blockchain for Every Species

Independent Species Chains

Each permitted species is represented by a singular append-only blockchain containing the ordered history of accepted submissions, corrections, observations, evidence, source imports, taxonomic changes, and validation decisions associated with that species.

Species chains remain independently addressable while sharing common protocol rules, schemas, cryptographic verification, peer discovery, and network transport.

The Complete Speciedex

The complete Speciedex is the verified union of all permitted species blockchains, taxonomic indexes, shared reference objects, manifests, source records, and protocol metadata.

A full-weight server node maintains a one-to-one, one-hundred-percent copy of this complete public corpus and can independently verify and serve it.

Data Lifecycle

Submission, Validation, and Consensus

Submit

Users, institutions, software clients, field devices, and approved provider adapters create signed data submissions using documented schemas.

Validate

The protocol performs structural, taxonomic, provenance, integrity, duplication, evidence, geographic, policy, and human-exclusion checks.

Review

Automated validation may be supplemented by peer review, expert review, source comparison, challenge periods, and conflict resolution.

Commit

Accepted records become ordered blocks or block entries in the appropriate species chain and propagate through SpeciedexNet.

Revise

Corrections do not silently erase history. Later accepted records supersede, qualify, dispute, or retract earlier assertions while retaining the complete audit trail.

Verify

Any node can verify hashes, signatures, ancestry, schema compatibility, source lineage, and current canonical state from the chain history.

Compensation

Bitcoin-Native Incentives

Data Bounties

Published tasks define required evidence, geographic scope, validation standard, deadline, reward, and dispute terms.

Valid Submissions

Eligible users may receive Bitcoin compensation for accepted observations, digitization, classification work, source recovery, translations, media, or field research.

Layer One

On-chain Bitcoin may be used for larger awards, grants, institutional work, escrowed programs, and durable settlement.

Lightning Network

Lightning may be used for smaller rewards, rapid payouts, recurring micro-bounties, API metering, and low-cost international settlement.

No Native Token

Speciedex does not require an altcoin, speculative token, or replacement monetary system. Bitcoin remains the sole monetary and store-of-value layer.

Transparent Terms

Reward eligibility, review state, funding source, fee policy, payout status, and final settlement references remain auditable without placing private participant data on species chains.

Homo sapiens Prohibition

Permanent Safety Boundary

Permanent Human Exclusion

Homo sapiens is forbidden from being entered as a species blockchain, taxon record, observation target, specimen subject, bounty subject, or derived profile.

The exclusion is constitutional to the protocol and is not an administrative preference that an operator may disable.

Boundary Enforcement

Validation rejects direct human records, aliases, disguised taxonomic entries, human biometric datasets, human genomic profiles, and records whose practical purpose is the tracking or profiling of people.

Incidental references to human activity may appear only as environmental context, provenance, authorship, conservation pressure, or interaction metadata attached to a permitted non-human species record.

Public Infrastructure

Open, Verifiable, and Reproducible

Open Interfaces

Documented schemas, APIs, feeds, exports, manifests, command-line tools, and reproducible queries allow independent software to use the corpus.

Source Provenance

Every imported or submitted assertion retains origin, license, retrieval date, transformation history, reviewer state, and supporting evidence.

Conflict Preservation

The system records disagreement and competing taxonomic opinions instead of converting uncertainty into a false single answer.

Long-Term Availability

Independent full-weight nodes, archival nodes, snapshots, removable-media packages, and content verification reduce dependence on any single organization.
