Public Web Access to the Speciedex Blockchain Dataset

SpeciedexWeb

SpeciedexWeb is the public website interface to the Speciedex blockchain dataset.

It is designed for people who need to view, explore, read, analyze, watch, compare, learn from, or export verified species data but cannot or will not operate a Speciedex node.

The website is served from SpeciedexCore gateway or full-weight nodes participating in SpeciedexNet. It does not centralize ownership of the dataset and does not replace independent verification.

Homo sapiens is not present and cannot be entered, searched, tracked, profiled, or represented as a species record.

Purpose

The Public Browser Gateway

Access Without Operating a Node

SpeciedexWeb gives users immediate browser access to verified Speciedex data when they cannot or do not wish to install, store, synchronize, maintain, or expose a node.

Backed by Real Nodes

The website reads from one or more SpeciedexCore gateway or full-weight nodes. It is an access service over the decentralized dataset, not a replacement for the peer-to-peer network.

Read and Explore

Users can view species profiles, chain history, taxonomy, observations, maps, media, conservation records, sources, citations, and network validation state.

Analyze and Learn

Web tools support filters, comparisons, timelines, educational views, dashboards, exports, and reproducible links within browser constraints.

Participation

Submission and Bounty Access

Web Submissions

Supported users may prepare structured observations, corrections, media, references, or bounty responses through guided forms and upload workflows.

Signed Handoff

Where stronger local signing or offline evidence handling is required, the website can hand work to SpeciedexCore or SpeciedexApp.

Bitcoin Rewards

Eligible accepted work may be compensated in Bitcoin on-chain or through the Lightning Network under the published bounty terms.

The website need not hold user funds. Payout addresses or Lightning invoices can be handled through documented, minimal-disclosure workflows.

Safety Boundary

Permanent Human Exclusion

Permanent Human Exclusion

Homo sapiens is forbidden from being entered as a species blockchain, taxon record, observation target, specimen subject, bounty subject, or derived profile.

The exclusion is constitutional to the protocol and is not an administrative preference that an operator may disable.

Boundary Enforcement

Validation rejects direct human records, aliases, disguised taxonomic entries, human biometric datasets, human genomic profiles, and records whose practical purpose is the tracking or profiling of people.

Incidental references to human activity may appear only as environmental context, provenance, authorship, conservation pressure, or interaction metadata attached to a permitted non-human species record.

Public Service

Privacy, Accessibility, and Performance

No Human Tracking

The site does not provide human species records, biometric lookup, person search, human location history, or human genomic profiles.

Sensitive Species Controls

Coordinates or details that could facilitate poaching, collection, harassment, or habitat damage may be generalized, delayed, or withheld.

Accessible Interface

Semantic structure, keyboard navigation, readable contrast, labels, reduced-motion support, captions, transcripts, and responsive layouts guide implementation.

Progressive Enhancement

Core reading and navigation remain functional with minimal JavaScript; advanced maps, playback, feeds, and analysis enhance the base document.

Caching and Resilience

Static assets, public records, and safe query results may be cached while visible freshness indicators prevent stale data from appearing current.

Rate and Abuse Controls

Public gateways protect node resources through reasonable limits, request validation, quotas, and abuse detection without altering the open dataset.
