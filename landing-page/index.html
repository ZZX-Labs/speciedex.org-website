The Open Species Index

An open, Bitcoin-native platform for documenting, preserving, and distributing biodiversity knowledge.

Speciedex is an open-source project dedicated to creating the world's most comprehensive digital index of known species. It combines modern taxonomy, scientific metadata, research references, media, geospatial information, and open standards into a freely accessible platform for researchers, educators, developers, governments, conservation organizations, and the public.

Inspired by the resilience, openness, and decentralization of Bitcoin, Speciedex is designed as long-term digital infrastructure. Our mission is to preserve biodiversity knowledge while making it universally accessible through open software, transparent standards, and reproducible scientific data.

Why Speciedex?

Humanity possesses an extraordinary amount of biological knowledge, yet much of it remains fragmented across countless databases, journals, institutions, museums, governments, universities, and private collections. Speciedex seeks to organize that information into an openly accessible, standardized, and verifiable ecosystem.

Rather than replacing existing scientific resources, Speciedex complements them by providing common infrastructure, shared metadata standards, open software, reproducible data, and permanent references linking researchers and institutions together.

Open Source

Every component is developed openly using transparent standards and permissive licensing.

Long-Term Preservation

Biodiversity information should remain available for generations, independent of any single organization.

Scientific Integrity

Every record can reference published literature, taxonomic authorities, genetic information, and other supporting evidence.

Community Driven

Scientists, institutions, developers, conservation organizations, and volunteers all contribute to the continuing improvement of the project.

Why Bitcoin?

Bitcoin provides a globally recognized foundation for open, decentralized, and censorship-resistant digital infrastructure. Speciedex leverages this philosophy and related technologies to improve the long-term preservation and integrity of scientific information.

Rather than treating biodiversity databases as isolated systems, Speciedex embraces open protocols, cryptographic verification, distributed infrastructure, and transparent development to maximize resilience and public accessibility.

Open Standards

Public specifications encourage interoperability across software, institutions, and research organizations.

Cryptographic Integrity

Metadata, releases, and datasets can be independently verified for authenticity and integrity.

Distributed Infrastructure

Multiple mirrors and independent operators improve reliability while reducing dependence upon any single provider.

Open Development

Every improvement can be inspected, reviewed, discussed, and reproduced by the global community.
