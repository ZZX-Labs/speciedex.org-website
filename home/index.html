Project Principles

The standards guiding the design, operation, and long-term development of Speciedex.

Open Access

Public biodiversity knowledge should remain accessible without unnecessary restrictions, proprietary lock-in, or dependence on a single institution.

Scientific Integrity

Records should be supported by traceable sources, taxonomic authorities, published research, reproducible methods, and transparent revision histories.

Long-Term Preservation

Species data, software, documentation, and research should remain available across generations, platforms, institutions, and changing technologies.

Independent Verification

Software releases, datasets, records, and metadata should be verifiable through published checksums, signatures, source references, and open specifications.

Interoperability

Open formats, stable identifiers, documented APIs, and common schemas enable collaboration between researchers, institutions, software systems, and public datasets.

Distributed Resilience

Mirrors, independent operators, local archives, and redundant infrastructure reduce dependence on any single server, organization, jurisdiction, or service provider.
